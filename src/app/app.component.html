Vibration Measurement

At GEARPRO, we are ISO 18436-2 certified from USA and helping our clients in solving the issues pertaining to gear box vibrations and helping them in operating their equipment without any unexpected catastrophic failure.

Measuring vibrations during the operation of a machine can yield valuable information about the dominant operating conditions or possible damages. During steady-state operation there is typically a periodic signal that can be broken down into the dominant frequencies via Fast-Fourier-Transformation (FFT). These frequencies can then be compared to the characteristic frequencies of the system’s components. The turning of a shaft leads to a vibrational excitation corresponding to the rotational frequency of the shaft or a whole number multiple thereof. Damage on a bearing ring leads to an excitation corresponding with the rolling element frequency that results from the rotational speed and bearing geometry. One can also consider the frequency of tooth engagement or the electrical supply.



Under non-steady-state conditions, i.e. changing rotational speeds and loads, FFT is generally not applicable. However, in these cases the peak values can be classified and counted. This applies to e.g. the bumps that a train car gearbox experiences when it changes tracks, goes over road crossings, or even drives over a track with imperfections. In this manner, sections can be identified that have an increased contribution to the deterioration of the mechanical structure. In various industrial machines such as rubber mixers and mills, the load peaks correspond strongly to the process parameters. In these cases, class counting can be used to optimize the process, allowing the demands on the mechanical structure to be reduced without sacrificing productivity.

Alignment Check & Correction

During operation due to the equipment vibrations, Anchor bolt toque issues, concrete settlement (in the green field projects), etc there is always a chance of developing misalignment between driver-driven equipment.

Operating gear boxes in misaligned conditions can induce forces on bearings and gear mesh and can cause severe mechanical stresses, which may lead pre-mature failure. To avoid any such failure its is recommended to check the alignment of the critical devices on regular maintenance schedules. At GEARPRO we have expertise and helping our client on aligning DRIVER-DRIVEN Machines.

Overhaul of the Gear box

Every gear and bearings are designed to only certain load carrying capacity and to certain life period. During operation on load Gear boxes under goes fatigue load. Fatigue loads on gear box internals will cause to contribute mechanical problems and leads to operational efficiency of the equipment and can also lead to catastrophic failure.

As per the design calculations, OEM’s recommends for overhaul of gear boxes after certain of operating hours. It is mandatory to follow OEM overhaul schedule and rebuilt the gear boxes. We, at GEARPRO have hands-on expertise experience and knowledge in rebuilding gear boxes for our clients with precision accuracy and helping our clients to finish their gear boxes rebuilt job on time.
